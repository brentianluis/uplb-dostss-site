Our History

In 1985, the National Science and Technology Authority (NSTA) scholars convened after realizing the need for an organization that will embody their ideals of maintaining academic excellence. Several meetings were held to discuss the plan regarding the establishment of a formal organization with its own constitution and set of by-laws. Soon after the ratification of the constitution, the UPLB NSTA Scholars’ Society was recognized as an academic organization on November 25, 1985.

The first president of NSTA-SS was Meinrado F. Samala. All scholars of NSTA were recognized as members of the newly formed society. They were active in participating in various quiz contests, which won them several awards. But this dynamism lasted only for a year.

In 1987, some of the charter members headed by Lope Doromal decided to revive the organization. This time it was named UPLB DOST Scholars’ Society (UPLB DOST-SS) under the new leadership of Anna Marie V. Magbanua.

To date, UPLB DOST SS has 49 resident members who pull their talents together in sponsoring activities that promote better understanding of science and technology in the spirit of service.
